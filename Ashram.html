About

Vanavasi Kalyan Karnataka :

Ashram was started In 1952 by Bala Sabji in Chattisgarh area in a place called Jayeshpur, which is present Head Quarter of our entire Organisation. In northern area they call as Kalyan Ashram, whereas in Karnataka they call as Vanvasi Kalyan Karnataka . Presently headed by Mr. Ramchandra Khrade. In Karnataka it was started in the year 1989 in Chipgeri in Uttara Kannada district.
                   The main person behind this was Mr, Prakash Kamath. And the idea was given by Mr. Ajit Kumar.They were Sangh Pracharaak, brought Vanvasi Kalyan asharam in Karnataka. Currently there are 9 different hostels in Karnataka out of these 2 are accommodated by girls and rest by the boys. The staff of the hostel take care of the childreb with all aspects. The expenses like accommodation , food ,medicine, education and fees are collected from the society people. The majority the collection is from the donors .The donations are in the 2 forms i.e Cash and Kind form like food items (rice, vegetables, fruits, pulses etc).

Chhattisgarh was considered a less developed region as it predominantly consisted of tribal communities. At that time, the Chief Minister of the region was Mr. V C Shukla and he expressed a desire to visit the Chhattisgarh area. However, upon attempting to enter the territory, he was met with resistance from the indigenous Adivasi community living in the region.. The government official, who was the Chief Minister of the region, encountered opposition from the Adivasi community when attempting to enter the Chhattisgarh area. The Adivasi community stated that they did not allow entry to anyone, including the government, as they did not allow the British to enter in the past. In response, the Chief Minister sent Ramakant Deshpande, a block officer from the Madhya Pradesh Government, to understand the problems faced by the tribal community. It should be noted that the community being referred to here are not classified as Scheduled Castes (SCs) but rather Scheduled Tribes (STs), and some groups are not officially recognized as tribals but still depend on the forest for their livelihood

The work we are doing is aimed at supporting various tribal communities, such as Dangar Gowli, Siddhi, Kumbli Marathi, Kulmbi, Valmikhis, and others in our district. This effort began in Chhattisgarh in 1952 when Mr. Ramakant Deshpande was given the responsibility to address the issues faced by these tribal peoples. When he approached the central government for funding, he was denied support. However, he received guidance from Madhav Sadashiv Golwalkar, also known as Guruji, who suggested that they should not depend solely on the government as governments may change, but instead work within the community, which is timeless. Guruji advised Deshpande to focus on engaging with the community and working with them directly rather than relying on government support

Services

MANE PATHA KENDRA

The government schools in the tribal areas face severe crunch of teachers with just 2-3 of them managing all the classes at a time. To ensure that the education of the Tribal Children is not hampered, VKK(RBN) conducts Evening Learning (Tution) Centres in Tribal areas. This is an after school programme where in the local educated tribal youths are appointed as teachers and they in turn are regularly provided the necessary training to teach the children.The Mane Patha Kendra is also a platform for students having a talent in sports or extra curricular activities. It has helped in increasing the confidence of students.

Bala Samskara Kendra

Here the children are given knowledge about our rich culture and varied heritage. They are taught the importance of values, virtues and ethics through shlokas, ancient Sanskrit epics and bhajans . An effort is made to imbibe the spirit of nationalism in the children through heroic tales of the freedom fighters of our country.

Hostel

Hostel facilities to vanavasi children to help then to get education and excel in various field by providing them proper environment in the form of accommodation, food, education, guidance, physical fitness, cultural and sports activities. Once the child joins the hostel, the entire expense of their education and accommodation is taken care by them.
